About Glaucoma

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, typically due to increased pressure within the eye. This damage can lead to vision loss and even blindness if left untreated. Glaucoma often develops gradually and without symptoms, making regular eye exams crucial for early detection and management. Treatment usually involves medications, laser therapy, or surgery to lower intraocular pressure and preserve vision.

It often develops slowly over time and without noticeable symptoms, which is why regular eye exams are crucial. However, some people may experience symptoms such as:

  1. Blurred vision
  2. Eye pain or redness
  3. Seeing halos around lights
  4. Tunnel vision
  5. Nausea or vomiting (especially in severe cases)

Early detection is the key to preventing vision loss from glaucoma. Treatment options vary depending on the type and severity of the condition, but may include:

  1. Eye drops to reduce intraocular pressure
  2. Oral medications to lower eye pressure
  3. Laser therapy to improve drainage of fluid from the eye
  4. Tunnel vision
  5. Surgery to create a new drainage pathway or implant a drainage device

It's important to consult an eye care professional for proper diagnosis and management if you suspect you may have glaucoma.
